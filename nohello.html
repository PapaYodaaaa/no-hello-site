Please don't just say "hello" in chat.

It's inefficient and wastes time. Just ask your question!

The Slow Way M-2

This is what happens when you just say "hi" and wait. It forces the other person to stop what they're doing just to reply "hello" so you can *then* ask your question.

You

Hi!

10:00 AM
Me

Hey, what's up?

10:05 AM
You

I have a quick question about the new project specs...

10:06 AM

...precious minutes wasted waiting.

The Better Way M-2

This is much better. You provide all the context and your question in one message. I can answer it as soon as I see it.

You

Hi! I have a quick question about the new project specs: where can I find the latest design mockups for the user dashboard?

10:00 AM
Me

Hey! You can find them all in the 'Designs' folder on the shared drive, here's the link: [link]

10:01 AM

...problem solved, in a fraction of the time!

Why is this better?

Respects Focus & Time

It allows the other person to answer when they have a moment of focus, without the pressure of a "hello" hanging over them. They can read, process, and answer all at once.

Enables Asynchronous Work

This is crucial for teams in different time zones. The person can wake up, see the full question, and answer it immediately, instead of waking up to "hi" and having to wait hours for the actual question.

Provides Full Context

By asking the full question, you're giving all the necessary information for the other person to help you. This prevents a long back-and-forth of clarifying questions.

It's Simply Faster

You get your answer faster. They spend less time context-switching. Everyone wins and can get back to doing meaningful work.

So, please...

Next time you need help, just send your full question. It's not rude, it's respectful of everyone's time.
