Are You Really Just Going to Say "Hello"?

It's a spectacular waste of everyone's time, including yours. Just ask the actual question.

The Clueless Way (Don't do this)

This is what happens when you just say "hi" and wait. It forces the other person to stop, breaking their focus, just to reply "hello" so you can *then* get around to asking your question. Brilliant.

You: hi
You: u there?
Them: hey, what's up?
You: I have a question about the new server config
Them: ok, what is it?

...all that time you'll never get back.

The Bare Minimum (Do this)

This is how functional adults communicate. You provide all the context and your question in one message. I can answer it and we can both move on with our lives.

You: Hey! Quick question: for the new server config, should I be using the `prod-v2` or `prod-legacy` environment variables?
Them: Hey, use `prod-v2`. The legacy one is being deprecated.

...problem solved, like magic. Or just... basic efficiency.

Why This Isn't Obvious is a Mystery

Respects Focus & Time

It allows the other person to answer when they have a moment of focus, without the lingering dread of a "hello" hanging over them. They can read, process, and answer all at once.

Works Across Time Zones

This is crucial for teams in different time zones. The person can wake up, see the full question, and answer it immediately, instead of waking up to a single, useless "hi" and having to wait hours for the actual question.

Provides Full Context

It gives the other person all the information they need to help you. They don't have to drag the details out of you one message at a time.

It's Simply Faster

You get your answer faster. They spend less time wondering what you want. Everyone wins and can get back to doing *actual* work.

So, Please. Just Ask.

Next time you need help, try using your words. All of them. Just send the full question. It's not rude, it's respectful of the fact that nobody has time for this.
